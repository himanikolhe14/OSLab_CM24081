Theory: Preemptive SJF

The Core Concept

Shortest Job First (SJF) is a scheduling policy that selects the waiting process with the smallest execution time to execute next. It guarantees the minimum average waiting time among all scheduling algorithms.

Preemptive Mechanics

Preemptive (SRTF): If a new process arrives with a shorter burst time than the remaining time of the current process, the CPU is taken away (preempted) and given to the new process.

Non-Preemptive: Once a process starts executing, it cannot be interrupted until it finishes its burst time.

Analysis & Conclusion

Advantages

  • Produces the optimal (minimum) average waiting time.
  • Excellent for systems where throughput is a priority.
  • Shorter jobs are executed quickly, freeing up memory.

Limitations

  • Starvation: Long processes may wait indefinitely if short ones keep arriving.
  • Requires precise prediction of burst times (difficult in reality).
  • High context switching overhead due to frequent preemptions.
